# Traditional Meihua Notes (Distilled)

This skill is inspired by 梅花易数, but it does **not** implement a full traditional hexagram-calculation engine.
It borrows a few stable principles and adapts them into a deterministic symbolic workflow.

## Core traditional anchors adopted here

### 1. 体为主，用为事
Traditional summaries often treat:
- **体** as the self / principal side
- **用** as the matter / counterpart / outer situation

In this skill, adapt that idea as:
- **主象 ≈ 体**
- **客象 ≈ 用 / 外势 / 近应**
- **变象 ≈ 后势 / 变势**

`阻象` and `应象` are modern scaffolding added for clearer AI reasoning.

### 2. 用为近应，互为中程，变为后势
A common traditional reading pattern is:
- 用卦更贴近眼前之事
- 互卦看事情中段与过程
- 变卦看事情后续与结果倾向

This skill simplifies that as:
- use `客象` to speak about **眼前怎么显**
- use `阻象` to represent **过程里最卡的中程结构**
- use `变象` to speak about **后势怎么转**

When writing `断势`, prefer a phased reading:
- 近势：看客象
- 中势：看阻象
- 后势：看变象

### 3. 不动不占，不因事不占
Traditional material often emphasizes:
- no needless divination
- one clear matter is better than a vague pile of worries

Apply this as a practical rule:
- prefer **一问一事**
- if the user mixes many topics, narrow to the dominant one first
- if the question is pure curiosity with no actual matter, keep the reading light

### 4. 吉凶悔吝生乎动
Traditional summaries often tie movement to manifestation and timing.

In this skill:
- **动** means the matter is active, pressing, or already entering expression
- **静** means the matter is latent, gathering, pausing, or not yet fully expressed

Do **not** turn this into fake precision timing.
Use it to discuss:
- urgency
- visibility
- manifestation speed
- whether to advance or observe

### 5. 动而吉凶，应验偏速；静而未应，端倪未全显
Traditional notes often associate movement with faster manifestation.

In this skill, adapt that idea modestly:
- if multiple key roles are **动**, the matter is more likely already unfolding
- if `客象` or `变象` is **动**, near-term changes are easier to observe
- if the frame is mostly **静**, speak in terms of slow emergence, delay, or “still gathering shape”

Again, do **not** force a fake exact date.
Prefer language like:
- 应得较快
- 近期见回音
- 中势见分晓
- 仍需等待其显化

## Simplified 体用生克 heuristics

Treat these as interpretive heuristics, not absolute fate-laws.

- **用生体**: outside conditions support the self / the matter benefits the querent
- **用克体**: outside pressure restrains the self / direct friction or suppression
- **体生用**: the self drains energy into the matter / over-investment or leakage
- **体克用**: the self can handle or push the matter, but often with cost
- **体用比和**: same tendency, resonance, sameness, reinforcement

## How this skill adapts them

Instead of speaking in rigid classical terms every time, translate them into readable Chinese:

- 用生体 → 得外势相生 / 外缘能扶主
- 用克体 → 外压制主 / 客来克体
- 体生用 → 主气外泄 / 心力耗于事中
- 体克用 → 能制其事 / 可推但费力
- 比和 → 同气相引 / 同势相叠

## Do not mechanize 生克

Traditional material also emphasizes distinctions of:
- 轻 / 重
- 真生真克 / 虚象之生克
- 旺 / 衰
- 动 / 静

This skill does not implement classical seasonal旺衰 calculation.
Instead it uses a simpler adaptation:

- `strength=强` → treat influence as more solid
- `strength=弱` → treat influence as lighter
- `dynamic=动` → treat influence as easier to manifest quickly
- `dynamic=静` → treat influence as latent or delayed
- `flow=顺` → treat the tendency as easier to consolidate
- `flow=逆` → treat the tendency as more折损 / more likely to stall

Think of it this way:
- 生克 gives **direction**
- 强弱 gives **weight**
- 动静 gives **speed of manifestation**
- 顺逆 gives **ease vs friction**

## Practical caution

Traditional sources often discuss exact应期, object classes, and image associations.
This skill does not attempt exact event-forecasting.
It focuses on:
- structure of the situation
- near / mid / later trend direction
- friction pattern
- manifestation speed
- suggested response
